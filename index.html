Ryan Thorngren

I'm a physicist with a background in mathematics, interested in symmetry, topology, and non-perturbative methods in many-body systems, from high energy to condensed matter.

I am currently at the KITP in Santa Barbara, in the northwest corner on the second floor. Starting in the Spring I will be a junior faculty at the IHES in France. We are hiring physics postdocs!

I am part of the Simons Collaboration on Global Categorical Symmetries.

Things I'm Thinking About

New boundary CFTs arising from anomalous symmetries, and intrinsically gapless topological phases.

Applications of Higgs-SPT ideas to detection of exotic superconductivity.

The "self-dual" critical point in the Fradkin-Shenker phase diagram.

Experimental observation of higher Berry phases.

Categorical symmetry in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions, and anomaly in-flow.

The spectrum of "quantum cellular automata", and placing the classification of SPTs on sound mathematical footing.
